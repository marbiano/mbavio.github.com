Educabilia

Learn new things everyday.

Educabilia is a latin-american startup who is giving people the power to teach what they know, and learn new exciting things everyday. I was asked to work on realigning the user interface, with special attention into making the new version responsive to different devices.

    Aug 2012 - Nov 2012

  • State

Old class page had a couple of usability problems, mostly it lacked of a proper visual hierarchy. We change the page to highlight class key information (date, location, modality) and we saw inmediately after how fast and easy was to read and undertand the new class page.

Home page & class search page were also updated, fixing usability issues and refreshing the general look & feel of them, to stay consistent with new design direction.

Right from the beggining, we worked with one concept as our guide for all the planning, design & build: content first. In a nutshell, we used real content and ideal line-length for our typography selections, to determine a page grid, and from there we made the site to respond based on how many grid columns a certain device could handle.

Tons of events are created on Educabilia each month, online & offline. That means hundreds of people learning at least one new thing each month. Talking about changing the world.

What tools do we use to build the front-end?

We did most of our sketches in paper, then moved to Balsamiq Mockups propotypes, and we ditched Photoshop for high fidelity comps, we designed in the browser directly.

The web app was built in a Ruby microframework called Cuba, and the front-end architecture was very customized, in order to improve speed of the site. No jQuery, just tiny js libraries each one doing one needed thing.

In order to build the front-end, we used semantic HTML5 for the markup, Sass + Compass + Susy for the visual styling, and as already mentioned, several small js libraries like Fayer, Bean, qwery and MicroAjax, among others. We also based our CSS architecture on concepts from SMACSS.
